## Executive Summary

The geopolitical landscape of the Western Hemisphere underwent foundational transformation in early January 2026 as the United States initiated direct and assertive management of Venezuelan state institutions following the removal of Nicolás Maduro from power.[[1]](#ref1)[[2]](#ref2)

### Key Timeline Events

**January 3, 2026**: U.S. special operations forces executed **Operation Absolute Resolve**—a high-precision strike in Caracas resulting in:
- Capture of Nicolás Maduro and wife Cilia Flores
- Transport to New York for federal narco-trafficking and corruption charges[[3]](#ref3)[[4]](#ref4)

**Post-Operation Vacuum**: Trump administration asserted U.S. would "run" the country until:
- Infrastructure stabilization
- Oil industry restoration to global market participation[[1]](#ref1)[[5]](#ref5)

**January 11, 2026**: Transition crystallized into **dual-layered governance structure**:
- **U.S. Administrative Oversight**: Energy and finance focus
- **Domestic Interim Government**: Led by Delcy Rodríguez (former Vice President), sanctioned by Supreme Tribunal of Justice[[2]](#ref2)[[3]](#ref3)

### The "Donroe Doctrine"

> **Definition**: Melding of 19th-century regional assertions (Monroe Doctrine) with 21st-century energy dominance strategies[[5]](#ref5)

**Radical Departure**: Instead of "maximum pressure" total purge, U.S. opted to work with "remnants of the Maduro regime," prioritizing:
- Operational stability
- Immediate resumption of oil exports
- Over rapid democratic transition[[1]](#ref1)[[6]](#ref6)

**Strategic Objectives**:
- Secure U.S. access to world's largest proven oil reserves
- Manage migration and security risks inherent in failed state[[5]](#ref5)[[6]](#ref6)

**Current Volatility** (as of Jan 11, 2026): Rodríguez administration balances U.S. demands against maintaining loyalty of still-armed Chavista military apparatus.[[6]](#ref6)

## 1. Institutional Management Roadmap and State of Advancement

### Three Primary Objectives

As of January 11, 2026, the U.S. roadmap (articulated by Energy Secretary Chris Wright and Secretary of State Marco Rubio) treats Venezuelan state as **managed entity under temporary U.S. receivership**:[[7]](#ref7)

```
U.S. Roadmap Objectives:
│
├── 1. Immediate seizure and sale of blockaded energy assets
├── 2. Rehabilitation of critical oil and electricity infrastructure
└── 3. Establishment of controlled financial mechanism for state revenue
```

### Energy Management Protocol and Tanker Seizures

**Focus**: "Fast wins" to justify military expenditure of intervention[[6]](#ref6)

**January 7, 2026 Actions**:
- U.S. forces seized two oil tankers (including one Russian-flagged)
- Target: Secure approximately **30-50 million barrels** of crude oil blockaded in Caribbean[[4]](#ref4)

**Department of Energy (DOE) Strategy**:
- Sell volumes in global markets "indefinitely"
- Proceeds deposited into **U.S.-controlled escrow accounts** at globally recognized banks[[4]](#ref4)[[7]](#ref7)

**Dual Purpose**:
1. Prevent illegitimate fund use by regime loyalists
2. Provide "seed funds" for new administration's essential services[[6]](#ref6)

### Energy Sector Status Matrix

| Component | Status as of January 11, 2026 | U.S. Strategic Objective | References |
|-----------|------------------------------|--------------------------|------------|
| **Blockaded Crude** | 30-50 million barrels seized or slated for seizure | Immediate liquidation to fund transition and pay U.S. creditors | [[4]](#ref4)[[7]](#ref7)[[6]](#ref6) |
| **Refineries** | Deteriorated; functioning at <20% capacity | Engineering assessments by U.S. firms; import of diluents to optimize flow | [[6]](#ref6)[[4]](#ref4) |
| **Electric Grid** | Subject to frequent nationwide blackouts | Targeted U.S. investment to support industrial and energy hubs | [[7]](#ref7) |
| **Export Strategy** | 80% previously destined for China | Redirection of exports exclusively to U.S. partners and Gulf Coast refineries | [[7]](#ref7)[[6]](#ref6) |

### Oil Company Contracts and Corporate Re-entry

**Current Status**: High-level negotiation phase

**Trump Administration Promise**: U.S. energy companies will return to "make the people of Venezuela rich"[[3]](#ref3)[[6]](#ref6)

**Reality for Major IOCs**: Complex landscape requiring:

#### Primary U.S. Operator
- **Chevron**: Remains significant presence
- **Rodríguez Administration Model**: Future agreements mirror existing Chevron partnership (U.S. firm handles commercialization)[[4]](#ref4)[[7]](#ref7)

#### Cautious Major Players
**ExxonMobil & ConocoPhillips** remain wary, seeking:
- Clear legal protections regarding 2007 expropriation claims
- Massive capital commitment safeguards[[4]](#ref4)

**Potential Solutions Under Consideration**:
- Specialized U.S. government-administered mechanism to process claims
- "Sanctions certainty" for large-scale investment[[4]](#ref4)[[6]](#ref6)

## 6. Strategic Synthesis: Does Regime Continuity Strengthen or Weaken U.S. Leverage?

### The Central Question

As of January 11, 2026, the U.S. administration faces critical assessment: Does maintaining "remnants" of Maduro regime—specifically the **Rodríguez-Padrino-Cabello axis**—strengthen or weaken long-term leverage?[[6]](#ref6)

---

### ✅ The Case for Strengthened Leverage

#### 1. The Ultimate Lever: Oil Revenue Control

**Mechanism**: De facto administrative takeover of oil sales[[7]](#ref7)

**Impact**: By controlling purse strings of country entirely dependent on oil revenue, U.S. gained **unprecedented compliance** from interim government

**Evidence**: Delcy Rodríguez, despite sovereignty rhetoric, forced to "bow economically" to U.S. demands regarding:
- Production-sharing
- Financial oversight
- To prevent total state failure[[13]](#ref13)

#### 2. Individual Compliance Incentives

**Threat Vector**: Further military action or personal prosecution against already-indicted leadership (Padrino, Cabello)

**Result**: Creates powerful individual incentive for cooperation[[10]](#ref10)[[16]](#ref16)

---

### ⚠️ The Case for Weakened Leverage

#### 1. "Pliable Client" Risk

**Problem**: Working with same repressive apparatus that oversaw country's collapse[[1]](#ref1)

**Consequence**: U.S. may inadvertently **insulate these figures from democratic accountability** demanded by Venezuelan opposition[[6]](#ref6)

#### 2. Stability vs. Democracy Trade-off

**Indicator**: Downplaying of Maria Corina Machado's movement

**Implication**: U.S. prioritizing "stability" over "democracy," potentially leading to long-term **legitimacy crisis**[[6]](#ref6)

#### 3. Failed State Guarantor Risk

**Scenario**: If interim government fails to provide internal stability (something old regime notoriously unable to do)

**Outcome**: U.S. seen as guarantor of failed but "friendly" dictatorship

**Strategic Impact**: Ultimately **weakens moral and strategic leverage** in region[[1]](#ref1)[[6]](#ref6)

---

### Final Assessment (January 11, 2026)

> **Current Status**: The roadmap is a work of **high-stakes improvisation**

**Reality Check**: U.S. "management" of Venezuela is currently:
- ✓ Successful military operation
- ❓ Looking for sustainable political and economic strategy

**Critical Period Ahead**: Coming weeks will determine if "Donroe Doctrine" can successfully transition from:

```
Current State           →    Desired End State
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━    ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Seizing tankers              Rebuilding a nation
```

The intervention's ultimate success depends on whether **tactical military victory** can evolve into **strategic governance transformation**.[[5]](#ref5)[[6]](#ref6)
